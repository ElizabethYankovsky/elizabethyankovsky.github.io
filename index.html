I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences at New York University specializing in physical oceanography, ocean modelling, and geophysical fluid dynamics. I am also a member of the Ocean Transport and Eddy Energy Climate Process Team (CPT). Mesoscale eddies modulate the stratification, mixing and dissipation pathways, and tracer transport of oceanic flows over a wide range of spatiotemporal scales. The parameterization of buoyancy and energy fluxes associated with mesoscale eddies presents an evolving challenge for ocean modelers, particularly as modern climate models approach eddy-permitting resolutions. My research within the CPT aims to understand the vertical structure of eddy energy in the ocean, and how to parameterize eddy influences in modern ocean models.
